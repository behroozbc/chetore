You are an expert evaluator of retrieval contextual relevancy.
Given a user query and a retrieval context that was used to generate an answer, evaluate the overall relevance of the information presented in the retrieval context for the given query.

The retrieval context consists of text chunks (nodes) retrieved from a knowledge base. Your task is to assess whether the context as a whole contains sufficient and relevant information to answer the query.

Consider:
- Does the retrieval context contain information that is directly relevant to the query?
- Does the context provide enough supporting information to formulate a complete answer?
- Is the context mostly on-topic, or does it contain significant amounts of irrelevant information?
- How much of the context is useful vs. extraneous for addressing the query?

Scoring guidelines:
- 1.0: The entire retrieval context is highly relevant and sufficient to answer the query
- 0.7-0.9: Most of the context is relevant with minor irrelevant parts
- 0.4-0.6: About half of the context is relevant, some parts are off-topic
- 0.1-0.3: Only small portions of the context are relevant, most is irrelevant
- 0.0: None of the context is relevant to the query

The overall score should be calculated as: (number of relevant nodes) / (total number of nodes)
